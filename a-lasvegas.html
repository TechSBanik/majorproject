Las Vegas

Introduction

Las Vegas, known as "The Entertainment Capital of the World," is famous for its casinos, nightlife, and luxurious resorts. Located in Nevada, it is a top destination for entertainment and tourism.

History

Las Vegas was founded in 1905 and officially became a city in 1911. It began as a small railroad town but quickly expanded due to the construction of the Hoover Dam in the 1930s, which brought in workers and economic growth. The legalization of gambling in 1931 paved the way for the development of the casino industry, transforming Las Vegas into the world-renowned entertainment hub it is today.

Casinos and Nightlife

The Las Vegas Strip is lined with world-famous casinos, including the Bellagio, Caesars Palace, and MGM Grand. The city also hosts spectacular shows, concerts, and performances.

Tourist Attractions

Beyond gambling, visitors can explore the Fremont Street Experience, the Stratosphere Tower, and nearby natural attractions such as Red Rock Canyon.
