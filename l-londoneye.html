London Eye

Introduction

The London Eye, also known as the Millennium Wheel, is a giant Ferris wheel on the South Bank of the River Thames in London. It is one of the most famous attractions in the United Kingdom.

History

The London Eye was officially opened to the public on March 9, 2000. It was designed by Marks Barfield Architects and originally intended as a temporary attraction but has since become a permanent fixture of London's skyline.

Architecture and Features

The structure stands 443 feet (135 meters) tall, with a wheel diameter of 394 feet (120 meters). It has 32 sealed and air-conditioned capsules, each representing a London borough, and can carry up to 800 passengers at a time.

Tourism

The London Eye offers breathtaking panoramic views of London, with landmarks such as Big Ben, St. Paul's Cathedral, and Buckingham Palace visible from its highest point. It is one of the most visited paid tourist attractions in the world.
