Yellowstone National Park

Introduction

Yellowstone National Park, established in 1872, is the first national park in the world. Located in Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho, it is known for its geothermal features, diverse wildlife, and stunning landscapes.

History

Yellowstone has been inhabited for thousands of years by Indigenous peoples, including the Shoshone. European-American exploration began in the early 19th century, leading to its designation as a national park in 1872 by President Ulysses S. Grant. This historic decision set a precedent for national parks worldwide.

Geothermal Features

Yellowstone is home to over 10,000 geothermal features, including the famous Old Faithful geyser and the Grand Prismatic Spring. These natural wonders are powered by a massive underground supervolcano.

Wildlife

The park is a sanctuary for wildlife, including bison, wolves, elk, and bears. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and scenic drives through the vast wilderness.
